## 4. [[V1] Remove pin_memory() in async_copy_to_gpu to fix sporadic stalls](https://github.com/vllm-project/vllm/pull/37006)


### Base Information

- **PR Number:** #37006
- **Author:** [sbeurnier](https://github.com/sbeurnier)
- **Merged By:** [njhill](https://github.com/njhill)
- **Merged time:** 2026-03-13 18:37:33
- **Type:** `SUBSTANTIAL`
- **[Changed Files](https://github.com/vllm-project/vllm/pull/37006/files) (1):**
  - `vllm/v1/worker/gpu/buffer_utils.py`

### Summary

**What changed and why**  
Removed the explicit `pin_memory()` call in `async_copy_to_gpu` and replaced it with a direct `out.copy_(x, non_blocking=True)`. This change addresses sporadic stalls under high-concurrency workloads by eliminating contention on the CUDA driver's global lock during `cudaHostAlloc()` and avoiding an extra memory copy.

**Technical impact**  
The change reduces latency and improves scalability in concurrent scenarios by leveraging the CUDA driver's built-in handling of pinned memory transfers. It eliminates a serialization bottleneck and the two-step copy (pageable→pinned→GPU), resulting in more predictable performance and higher throughput, especially for larger tensors.

**Potential risks**  
If the input tensor `x` is not already in pinned memory, the direct copy may still incur a one-time pinning cost, though the driver manages this more efficiently. There is a slight risk of increased latency for very small tensors in low-concurrency edge cases, but the benchmarks show overall improvement.

**Key insights**  
Explicit `pin_memory()` calls can introduce significant overhead under concurrency due to driver-level locking. Relying on the CUDA driver's automatic transfer optimization is preferred for performance-critical paths. Developers should validate similar patterns elsewhere in the codebase and consider concurrency-aware benchmarking to catch such issues early.
